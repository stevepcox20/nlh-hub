How This Manual Works

The operational core. Principles over protocols.

This is the operational core of the NLH coaching practice. The other manuals (Philosophy & Psychology, Metabolism & Quantum Biology, Relational Coaching, Money) give the why, the what-is-happening-biologically, and the where-it-shows-up. Built on a single premise: a coach who works from principles can do what a coach who only works from protocols cannot. Protocols break when clients do not fit the script. Principles let you build a new tool, in real time, for the client in front of you.

03

Two Foundational Stances

The attentional posture held across all the work. Not optional.

The watcher is how the client meets what arises. Dimensionalising is what they do once they meet it.

The Watcher Stance

The dis-identified observer position. "I notice anger here", not "I am angry." "I am watching this memory with curiosity", not "this memory is overwhelming." The shift is from being-the-experience to being-the-one-watching-the-experience. It is what makes the deeper subconscious work safe. Without it, retrieval risks re-traumatisation. With it, retrieval becomes the doorway to updating.

BMR application: this is what Chapter 10 practice builds in real time. What am I about to do? What do I usually do next? What would my Next Level Self do instead? The watcher in the moment before the old path activates.

Dimensionalising Consciousness

Treating internal experience as if it were a separate entity with its own location, characteristics, voice, and life. The coach asks the client to locate a feeling in the body. Give it a colour. A texture. A temperature. A weight. A shape. A personality. A voice. Once dimensionalised, the client is no longer fused with it. They are in relationship with it.

This is what makes the MUD Rewrite process feel different from journalling. The client is meeting the pattern as an entity, not analysing it. That distinction produces the identity-level prediction error the assembly needs to destabilise.

One doorway usually presents first in a session, the body knot, the emotion that surfaces on induction, the memory that arises with the music. The coach follows what arises. The eight categories are a map so the coach knows what they are looking at. Both stances are required: without the watcher the client fuses with what surfaces; without dimensionalising the material has nowhere to go.

Every human runs the same cascade, top to bottom:

Upper three (S, I, G) are "the dial", where coaching enters. Lower three (N, A, L) are "the room", the downstream physiology where the dial's setting expresses in the body.

The one-sentence NLH: move the SIGNAL at its source. You do not fight the room. You turn the dial.

Vapor, Water, Ice: the three phases of self

These are not separate domains. They are layers of the same person. A change at the vapor layer that does not express at the ice layer is incomplete. A change at the ice layer with no vapor-layer authoring behind it tends not to last.

MUD: Misguided Unconscious Decisions

The substance of the patterns being worked with. Composition: story (the meaning the system has made of an experience) plus emotion (the charge that fused to the story under the stress that originally formed it).

  • Story = the mud itself
  • Emotion = the rebar
  • Stress at the moment of formation = the trowel that pushed them together

Once formed, MUD operates as a filter. The system perceives and responds to the present through the lens of the MUD laid down in the past. The client experiences this as automaticity: patterns they cannot interrupt, reactions they cannot choose otherwise, beliefs that feel like facts.

05

The Science of Change

Part I. Why willpower fails and what actually updates a pattern.

We are not suppressing patterns. We are not overriding them. We are working within the reconsolidation window to update the underlying assembly itself.

Hebb's Law

"Neurons that fire together, wire together." (Hebb, 1949; Caporale & Dan, 2008). When two neurons are repeatedly activated in close temporal proximity, the synaptic connection between them strengthens. Repeated co-activation produces a stable neural assembly, a network that fires as a unit whenever any part of it is triggered. This is the substrate of habit, belief, identity, and pattern.

Change is not about willpower or insight alone. It is about changing the assembly itself. That requires more than a decision. It requires the conditions under which assemblies become flexible.

Memory Reconsolidation: the window of change

(Nader, Schafe & LeDoux, 2000). When a consolidated memory is retrieved, it returns to a transient unstable (labile) state. During this labile period the memory can be modified before being re-stabilised.

Mechanically: retrieval triggers exchange of AMPA receptor subunits at the synapse. Calcium-impermeable receptors swap for calcium-permeable ones, destabilising the synapse. Protein synthesis is then required to re-stabilise the memory. If new learning occurs during the labile window, the memory reconsolidates with the new information incorporated. The original neural assembly is not erased. It is updated.

The window is narrow, typically 4 to 6 hours. Not every retrieval opens it. The memory must be sufficiently activated, but the activation must produce some mismatch with expectation.

Bruce Ecker's 2024 review of over 30 reconsolidation studies concluded: when retrieval, prediction error, and new learning are all properly engaged, behavioural updating of emotional memory is robust and replicable. When any of those three conditions fails, the intervention fails.

Prediction Error: the trigger that opens the window

Prediction error is a mismatch between what the brain expected (based on the retrieved memory) and what is actually occurring in the present moment. If the present confirms the memory's predictions, the pattern is reinforced. If it contradicts them, prediction error signals the brain that the existing model is inadequate, and the brain initiates updating.

The four channels

  • Somatic: body expected one state, produces another. The watcher stance and parasympathetic dominance interrupt the autonomic sequence the assembly predicts.
  • Cognitive: mind expected one meaning, encounters another. "This is dangerous" is met with "what is its lesson? What are you here to teach?"
  • Perceptual: senses expected the same configuration, register another. Different breath, music, posture, eye mask. Sensory context contradicts where the pattern was reinforced.
  • Identity: the deepest channel. The assembly assumed identification: "this is me." The watcher stance reveals "I have this pattern, I am not this pattern." Dimensionalising: "this is an entity in the room with me, but it is not me."

DEEP and QEC are constructed to generate prediction error simultaneously across multiple channels. DELTA and QAT generate it more diffusely, establishing brain-wide states that contradict the assumptions of nearly every existing assembly at once.

Unwind and New Wind

When a memory is retrieved and labilised, it is going to reconsolidate. The question is what it reconsolidates as. If the old story, old emotion, old reactivity is re-experienced in its old form, the assembly stabilises back into the same configuration. You unwound it, and then it rewound itself.

Frankensteining the pattern: opening the window and then sewing the same thing back together, sometimes with extra reinforcement.

The principle: unwind through retrieval and prediction error, then, within the labile window, deliberately introduce different content. New emotional, cognitive, somatic, and identity content. The assembly reconsolidates around what you put in during the labile window. When a coach skips the new wind, they are doing reactivation work, not reconsolidation work. The outcomes are not the same.

Metaplasticity: the state that makes change possible

Metaplasticity is plasticity about plasticity: the brain's capacity to modulate its own ability to undergo plastic change (Abraham & Bear, 1996). Not all brain states are equally receptive.

Reduces metaplastic capacity
Increases metaplastic capacity
Stress, rigid focus, sympathetic dominance, threat detection
Safety, parasympathetic tone, diffuse awareness, integration across networks

This is the missing concept in most coaching and therapy. Practitioners who do reframing or somatic activation without preparing the metaplastic ground are doing precise interventions in soil that cannot hold them. DEEP and QEC do specific reconsolidation. DELTA and QAT establish and maintain the metaplastic ground.

Specific reconsolidation without metaplastic conditioning = fragile change. Metaplastic conditioning without specific reconsolidation = a flexible brain that has not updated its operative maladaptive patterns. Both are required.

Attention, Function & Sequencing

Part I continued. FA vs OM, the two functions, prime-work-seal, brainwaves.

Focused Attention vs Open Monitoring

Focused Attention (FA): direct, narrow, sustained attention onto a specific object. Activates dorsolateral prefrontal cortex, anterior cingulate, task-positive networks.

Open Monitoring (OM): broad, non-selective awareness in which whatever arises is observed without targeting. Activates the Default Mode Network and posterior integrative regions. (Lutz, Slagter, Dunne & Davidson, 2008)

Focused AttentionChannel-based (tune to one station)DEEP, QEC, BEEPSpecific reconsolidation, precision work on a targeted assembly

Every BRN protocol does one of two things.

Skipping the seal: the reconsolidation window is open and the new encoding is partially in, but the consolidation that locks it into a durable assembly requires hours of parasympathetic recovery. Go straight back into stress and it does not hold.

Brainwaves and what they correlate with

The hypnagogic state is the target. Theta is its brainwave correlate. The specific mechanism is theta oscillations with gamma bursts nested within them (theta-gamma phase-amplitude coupling). The hippocampus rides the theta rhythm, the gamma carries the specific content, and the coupling between them is what allows the retrieved assembly to enter the labile state where it can be updated. (Bellfy, Smies & Kwapis, 2020; Headley & Pare, 2017)

The subjective experience is primary. Brainwave language is a useful map. The phenomenological markers (dreamlike imagery, dialogues with parts, symbolic surfacing, the sense of being neither fully here nor fully gone) are the actual signal a coach is reading.

Sympathetic and parasympathetic in reconsolidation

MUD forms under sympathetic activation. The original encoding happens in fight, flight, or freeze. To bring it back to the labile state, the same activation level needs to be present. The activating breath in DEEP recreates sympathetic activation without requiring actual danger. What follows matters as much as the activation itself: the shift into parasympathetic dominance, through the breaking breath, humming exhale, and vagal activation, is what allows the labile assembly to reconsolidate around the new content rather than the old.

The Five Analytical Questions

Ask these of any intervention. If it cannot answer them, it may not be doing what it claims.

These five questions can be asked of any meditative, somatic, journaling, breathwork, or contemplative intervention. A coach who can answer them about any tool understands it well enough to use it intelligently. Run any module, worksheet, video, or practice prescription for Alex through them before delivery.

#QuestionWhat it evaluates
1RetrievalWhat does this intervention retrieve? Is a specific memory, pattern, or assembly being activated, or is it working at a global state level?
2Prediction errorDoes it generate prediction error, and in which channel: somatic, cognitive, perceptual, or identity?
3New encodingWhat new content does it encode during the reconsolidation window: somatic-emotional, cognitive-relational, or perceptual?
4Metaplastic conditioningDoes it support it? Is it activating the DMN, inducing parasympathetic dominance, supporting open-monitoring states?
5SequencingWhere does it sit in a sequencing structure: priming, working, or sealing?

BRN is not a collection of techniques. It is an applied neuroscience of belief, identity, and pattern. Every protocol is an expression of the same underlying mechanisms: neurons that fire together wire together, memories destabilise on retrieval, prediction error opens the window, new encoding during that window updates the assembly, and metaplastic conditions determine whether the change takes and holds.

Breath as Subconscious Lever

Part II. The fastest, cheapest, most reliable state tool, and the one the client keeps.

Breath is the fastest, cheapest, and most reliable tool for shifting a client's state, and the only one the client can take with them. It sits at a unique anatomical location: one of the only autonomic functions the conscious mind has direct voluntary access to. Breath is the doorway through which the conscious mind reaches into the autonomic system.

The Two Breaths

BreathPatternEffect and use
ActivatingDouble inhale through nose or mouth (belly, then chest), followed by a passive exhale that is allowed to fall, not pushedSustained inspiratory drive signals effort and demand. Heart rate, sympathetic tone, and cortical arousal rise. Used to retrieve charged material in its original somatic-emotional signature.
BreakingDeep inhale, then a long, slow, controlled exhale, often paired with a hum (the ari or bee breath)Slow exhalation activates the vagus nerve and triggers parasympathetic dominance. Heart rate and cortical arousal drop, the amygdala downregulates. The hum increases nasal nitric oxide and adds vagal stimulation via the laryngeal branch. This is home base, the resourcing breath taught before any subconscious work begins.

Contraindications for the activating breath: cardiovascular disease (coronary artery disease, prior MI, congestive heart failure, uncontrolled hypertension), active or recent stroke or TIA, pregnancy (especially second and third trimesters), active psychosis, severe dissociative disorder or unstabilised PTSD, glaucoma or conditions where increased intraocular pressure is contraindicated, recent thoracic or abdominal surgery, epilepsy or seizure disorder. When in doubt, default to the coherence breath. The work can still be done.

Rest-based breathing: non-negotiable safety architecture

In every protocol that uses the activating breath, the structure is rest-based. Push until you cannot, rest until you can go again. The pacing is governed by the client's body, not the coach's prompts. This prevents hypocapnia, the drop in blood CO2 that causes vasoconstriction, lightheadedness, tingling, and syncope in extreme cases.

The Coherence Breath

Six-second inhale paired with a six-second exhale, no holds, sustained as a continuous rhythm. Produces respiratory sinus arrhythmia at its maximal coherence point. Measurably improves heart-rate variability, reduces stress markers, produces a balanced autonomic state. (McCraty & Childre, 2010; Lehrer & Gevirtz, 2014)

Two clinical uses: (1) the alternative for clients who cannot do the activating breath, since all BRN protocols can be run with coherence breath substitution; (2) the breath of integration and reflection, the operating breath of the integration phases and the standalone homework breath.

Music pairing: clinical, not atmospheric

Music is a second instrument played alongside the coach's voice. It does autonomic work the voice cannot do alone.

Activation music: intense, driving, percussive, fast-paced, rhythmic. Drum-heavy, synth-driven cinematic builds. No lyrics, no recognisable melody. Drives sympathetic engagement.

Rest music: soft, spa-like, sustained instrumental. Solfeggio frequencies (432 Hz, 528 Hz, 963 Hz) or rotated across rest segments. Pads, bowls, soft strings, ambient drones. Nothing percussive. Holds the parasympathetic state in which reconsolidation completes.

Music rules (non-negotiable)

  • No lyrics. Words compete with the coach's voice and fire memory associations.
  • No popular or familiar music. Any recognised track activates associations that hijack the protocol.
  • Two distinct phases: activation for sympathetic drive, rest for parasympathetic seal.
  • Volume calibration: activation loud enough to push but not to drown the voice; rest quiet enough that silences feel like silence.

Vocalization, sound, and the biofield

Humming reliably increases nasal nitric oxide, activates the vagus nerve via the laryngeal branch, and produces parasympathetic dominance. Studies of OM chanting show decreased limbic activity and increased heart-rate variability. (Eby, 2006; Kalyani et al., 2011)

Clinical uses in BRN: the hum on the breaking breath; sustained chanting tones in some QEC variations (root through crown: uh, oh, ah, ay, ee, ohm, mmm); an OM-style sustained tone at the end of QAT.

Breath-to-state mapping

State the protocol needsHow breath produces it
Sympathetic activation for retrieval (DEEP activation phase, BEEP, QAT induction rounds)Activating breath, rest-based, paced with intense rhythmic music
Parasympathetic dominance for integration and sealBreaking breath with humming exhale
Coherent reflective state for journaling and integrationCoherence breath (6 in, 6 out)

DEEP & BEEP

Part III. The most intensive specific-reconsolidation protocol, and its daily-practice version.

DEEP is the developmental edit of the document. QEC is the line edit. Both are specific-reconsolidation protocols, channel-based work, primarily at the vapor layer (story-belief-identity), with secondary work at the water layer (emotional charge).

DEEP: Depth Enhanced Emotional Processing

The most somatically and psychologically intensive specific-reconsolidation protocol in the BRN system. Uses breath, music, dimensionalised consciousness, and journaling stacked on each other. Approximately 55 minutes.

Protocol structure

Prep. Then a session of five phases: Induction, Activation (BEEP-style breath sequence), Transition Hold, Compassion Integration, Sonic Rest. Then journaling in three voices.

The Three Voices journaling

  • Voice One: The Psychic Entity Speaks (5 min): the client writes as the entity that came up (the memory, sensation, emotion, pattern, or part). Where it lives, what it looks like, what it has been trying to say. Full retrieval into the labile window through the writing channel.
  • Voice Two: The Current Self Responds (4 min): the client writes back to the entity. Not blaming, not whimpering, not denying. Just telling their truth about how it has been to live with this.
  • Voice Three: The Next-Level Human Self Writes (5 min): the future, wiser self sits down with both. Explains the lesson. Explains why it had to be this way. Encodes the meaning-rich, purpose-rich frame that becomes the new content reconsolidated into the assembly.

DEEP through the Five Analytical Questions

  • Retrieval: specific charged assembly, full somatic-emotional signature via activating breath plus dimensionalising.
  • Prediction error: all four channels at once. Somatic (body breathes through rather than reacts), cognitive (threat becomes teacher), perceptual (music, eye mask, headphones), identity (dimensionalising breaks identification).
  • New encoding: the Compassion Integration phase encodes love, acceptance, and gratitude while the assembly is still labile. Three Voices journaling encodes the lesson and next-level frame.
  • Metaplastic conditioning: Sonic Rest and Compassion Integration produce extended parasympathetic dominance and DMN engagement.
  • Sequencing: the work phase. Optimally primed by DELTA, sealed by DELTA in the days following.

Indications: a specific, identifiable charged memory, traumatic residue, or persistent emotional-somatic pattern that cognitive work alone has not resolved. Once weekly is standard frequency.

The Two Paths teaching

DEEP closes with this. The client did not erase the old path. They cannot. What they did is create a fork in the road. The old path, familiar and frustrating, is still there. The new path, foreign and fearful, is now also there. The teaching is to choose the foreign one. The foreign and fearful path turns into the foreign and fun path. The more you choose it, the more it becomes the new familiar.

BEEP: Breath Enhanced Emotional Processing

The practice-level extension of DEEP. Approximately 11 minutes. Same breath-driven retrieval mechanism without the full multi-phase architecture. No extended Compassion Integration, no long Sonic Rest, no Three Voices journaling. Structurally: DEEP's activation phase plus a brief integration. Prescribed as homework between DEEP sessions, used inside a one-on-one when a client needs quick subconscious access, and the coach's own daily nervous-system maintenance practice.

QEC & Choosing DEEP vs QEC

Part III continued. The inquiry-driven protocol and the selection rule.

QEC: Quantum Energy Clearing

A sustained-attention specific-reconsolidation protocol. Does not use breath-driven state induction. Uses focused inquiry sequentially across body regions, energy centres, memories, or patterns. Where DEEP is intensity-driven, QEC is inquiry-driven. Approximately 30 to 40 minutes.

Structure: seven energy centres (root through crown), each with 10 focused breaths into the area, three layered clearing statements (for MUD and beliefs, for history and memories, for sensations and illness), and three humming releases. The canonical version uses the chakra structure. The alternative uses anatomical body regions: pelvis, abdomen, upper abdomen, chest, throat, head, top of head.

Choose DEEP whenChoose QEC when
Specific, identifiable charged memory or patternPattern distributed across the body or multiple locations
Client can tolerate sympathetic activationClient cannot tolerate intense breathwork or has cardiovascular contraindications
Work needs full somatic-emotional retrieval intensitySystemic issues: chronic pain syndromes, energetic complaints
Single dominant themeClient prefers a gentler, more inquiry-based experience
Full 55-minute session available30 to 40 minutes and a complete protocol cycle available

Pairing DEEP and QEC: illustrative weekly schedule

  • Monday: DELTA (prime)
  • Tuesday: DEEP (heavy specific reconsolidation)
  • Wednesday: DELTA or BEEP (seal plus light maintenance)
  • Thursday: QEC (distributed-pattern inquiry-based reconsolidation)
  • Friday: QAT or DELTA (seal plus generative work if appropriate)
  • Weekend: rest. Optional Brain Breath or daily VIR for maintenance.

This structure is illustrative, not prescriptive. A client in acute distress should run DELTA daily until the nervous system stabilises, then add specific work in. The prescription is built for the client.

11

DELTA & QAT

Part IV. The soil. Everything that makes specific reconsolidation take and hold.

DEEP and QEC do the specific reconsolidation. DELTA and QAT do everything that makes it actually take and hold. They are not the supporting cast. They are the soil.

DELTA: Downregulation and Nervous System Restoration

The global metaplasticity and parasympathetic restoration protocol. Named for the delta-wave brain state it induces. The most accessible and repeatable of the four BRN classes. Approximately 22 minutes.

Three phases

  1. Extended body-scan, begins as focused-attention work and progressively diffuses into open-monitoring
  2. Box-breathing rhythm with explicit identity-dissolution language
  3. Reconstruction phase, seeds the new identity into the now-metaplastic state

DELTA through the Five Analytical Questions

  • Retrieval: minimal. DELTA is not a precision tool.
  • Prediction error: diffuse, identity-level. "You are not your body, you are not your emotions, you are not your thoughts" generates identity-level prediction error against the entire ego-assembly.
  • New encoding: non-specific identity-level seeding through reconstruction: "From this place of pure potential, begin to reassemble yourself. No old stories, no inherited scripts."
  • Metaplastic conditioning: maximal. This is what DELTA does best.
  • Sequencing: the prime and seal of the BRN system. Day before DEEP or QEC, day after, standalone daily.

FA-first then OM: recent research confirms focused-attention-first followed by open-monitoring is more effective than OM-first for novices, because FA establishes the attentional control that OM can then operate within (Ishikawa et al., 2025). Two versions exist, DELTA 1 and DELTA 2, same architecture and different language and tone, so the coach can vary delivery for clients running it regularly.

When to use DELTA: nervous system restoration, before and after DEEP or QEC, high-stress periods when specific work is contraindicated, clients with burnout or chronic stress or autonomic instability, and whenever the system needs rest more than targeted work.

QAT: Quantum Awareness Training

A global metaplasticity protocol that uses breath-driven absorption to access deep field-states. Where DELTA is restorative, QAT is generative. Where DELTA dissolves identity to allow rest and reconstruction, QAT dissolves identity to allow seeding of intention into the field-state. Approximately 30 minutes.

Structure: three rounds of accelerated breathwork (BEEP-style activation but more sustained), then into the void, identity dissolution and field-saturation. From the void an intention is projected into the field. It is received back through the heart centre, paired with elevated affective states (joy, gratitude, completeness). The protocol closes with the explicit framing that the chosen reality has already shifted.

QAT through the Five Analytical Questions

  • Retrieval: minimal. QAT is generative, not corrective. The void state explicitly empties prior content.
  • Prediction error: identity-level through deep dissolution into the void. "You are everywhere and nowhere, you are everyone and no one."
  • New encoding: forward-encoding of intention paired with elevated affect. The heart-opening phase produces the felt-sense conviction that the intention is already true.
  • Metaplastic conditioning: maximal, sometimes deeper than DELTA in some clients, paired with active intention.
  • Sequencing: flexible. Can serve as prime, work, or seal depending on the client and goals.

On the void: not a metaphor. A specific phenomenological state characterised by identity dissolution, content emptying, sustained background awareness without object, and a sense of vast openness. From this state the practitioner projects an intention, not a wish or fantasy but a clear thought-form, into the field. (Chowdhury et al., 2025; Sacchet et al., 2024)

When to use QAT: generative goals, manifestation work, possibility-state expansion, high-performance preparation, creative breakthrough, spiritual development. For clients already reasonably regulated and ready for deeper contemplative-grade practice. Less appropriate as first-line for clients in acute distress.

PairingWhy it works
DEEP + DELTADEEP does deep, intense, sympathetic-driven specific reconsolidation. DELTA does deep, restorative, parasympathetic-driven metaplastic conditioning. Exact complements. DELTA before DEEP establishes the metaplastic ground; DELTA after DEEP seals the new encoding. Together, the full architecture for serious heavy work.
QEC + QATQEC does sustained-attention specific reconsolidation. QAT does field-state generative metaplastic work. They share a meditative, contemplative register. QAT before QEC opens the field; QAT after QEC seals with intentional generative content. Well-suited for clients oriented toward inquiry, manifestation, and intentional life-direction work.

The Retrain Tools

VIR, WIR, and Brain Breath. The ice layer, and where Brain Breath correctly sits.

Retrain tools work at the ice layer: nervous system as physical actor, biology and behaviour. Where Rewire tools do field-state work at the water layer, Retrain tools build new lived patterns through repetition until the new path fires faster than the old one. These are not subconscious protocols. They are conscious rehearsal and nervous-system maintenance.

Why Brain Breath is Retrain, not Rewire: its mechanism is autonomic regulation, a sympathetic-activation-then-parasympathetic-shift cycle that resets the nervous system in three minutes. That is biology and behaviour at the ice layer, not field-state work at the water layer. The distinction matters for prescription. Brain Breath manages state; DEEP and QEC update assemblies.

The prescription frame for BMR

Build the metaplastic ground first (DELTA daily). Add specific reconsolidation once the nervous system is stable (DEEP or QEC weekly). Never skip the seal (DELTA or QAT after). BEEP and VIR are the between-session maintenance layer. The Vapor/Water/Ice frame maps the nine BMR outcomes: Trust Rebuilder, Honest Conversation, and Betrayal Trauma at the Rewrite layer; Brain Rewiring, Urge Response, and the pattern-navigation work at the Rewire layer; Success Blueprint, Recovery Game Plan, and Thriving Community at the Retrain layer.

Module Welcome: Science, Philosophy & Art

From the module videos. The continuum that guides the whole coaching craft.

"You and I could be teaching the exact same thing. They just could not hear it from me. They hear it from you. Your essential is matched to theirs."

This module teaches the coaching craft itself, arguably the most important module in the whole framework, because it is guided by three things at once: the science, the philosophy, and the art. Think of it as a continuum, not a hierarchy.

LayerWhat it isRole
ScienceThe frameworks, protocols, and logic-based tools that get measurable resultsWithout it, coaching is just making things up
PhilosophyThe NLH worldview (covered in full in the Psychology & Philosophy module)Sits in the middle. Anchors the science so it points somewhere, and evolves as understanding deepens
ArtIntuition, feel, insight, the things logic can't explainCan't be taught. Intuitive and insight-based, not intellect-based. Leaned into, not trained out

The warning built into this module

The coach is about to be handed a lot of frameworks: do it this way, here's the ABC, here's the one-two-three. Adhering to those exclusively costs the coach their heart, their intuitive sense, their ability to truly connect. That connection is exactly why a given client can hear something from one coach and not another, even teaching the identical content. There are people only you can reach.

Personal tools and interests a coach already carries (tarot, astrology, anything) are welcome in the work, but they have to fit inside the NLH science framework, not replace it, otherwise the results can't be measured. Neither side gets abandoned for the other: not art for science, not science for art. Both married is what a good coach does.

Story Circles, Sessions & Conversations

From the module videos. A distinct delivery format, not a variant of coaching. Facilitate a conversation or a witnessing.

"This is not coaching. Instead, what you are doing is facilitating a conversation, or facilitating a witnessing. In the authentic telling of the story of struggle, healing is done."

Where circles sit in the delivery menu

There are several distinct ways to deliver the Next Level Human methods, and the point of keeping them straight is so you can run different experiences and stack them in your business. A circle should never feel like a watered-down version of coaching. It is its own thing.

FormatWhat it is
One-on-onesIndividual coaching
Group coachingA completely different experience from 1:1
BRN classesBelief Repatterning and Neuroplasticity classes
Sessions / circles / conversationsThe subject of this training. Story and witnessing, not coaching

When you run this in your business it must feel distinct from group coaching, distinct from one-on-ones, distinct from classes. Its own experience.

Why it works: story, MUD, and being witnessed

The model takes its inspiration from 12-step (AA is the most famous). Strip the method down and the active ingredient is story, and being witnessed in the telling of it.

This lines up directly with NLH. MUD (misguided unconscious decisions) is made of story - the stories we tell are the mud we hold, and the mud we hold determines the identity and beliefs we hold. So giving someone the chance to tell their story authentically is not a soft add-on. In the authentic telling of the story of struggle, healing is done.

The clinical link is reconsolidation: retrieving a memory makes it labile, loosely held and changeable (see Section 05). When someone is prompted to tell the story slightly differently, naming the pattern, naming a gift, the story itself begins to change as they hold it. The circle is a reconsolidation event delivered through group witnessing rather than a protocol.

Two things you're actually doing (neither is coaching)

ModeWhat it looks like
Facilitating a conversationYou put a topic in the room ("let's talk about how telling our stories dissolves our patterns. What have you learned?"), invite named people to speak ("go ahead, Jane"), and let each person think out loud with no one interrupting. When they finish: "Does that remind anyone else of anything?"
Facilitating a witnessingThe story circle proper. People tell their SUG and are witnessed in the telling. You hold the space, keep time, and witness. You do not respond with coaching.
SUG StepWhat they tellWhy it's asked
StruggleThe authentic story, what happened, told straight without performanceThe raw material
UnderstandingHow it feels, what they've made it mean, the coping mechanisms and patterns they're noticingMoves them from event to pattern-recognition
GiftWhat they've learned, and if they wish, a word of wisdom for othersMost people don't see a gift unfacilitated. Naming one is the reconsolidation move: it changes how the story is held, not just how it's told. Prompt for it: "if someone else was just beginning this struggle, what would you tell them?"

The opening script (read aloud)

There is a handout for this in the download section below the video. The coach reads it aloud at the open:

"Welcome to Story Circles. Let's remember, this is not about coaching, advice, or therapy. This is a place for you to tell your story and be witnessed. Some of you are here just as witnesses. Thank you for being here, you may not tell your story. Some of you are here to share. I'm so excited you're here, thank you for sharing. Each of you will have about three to seven minutes. I'll keep time, and if you start going over I'll gently nudge you. Remember the format: tell us your struggle, what happened, your authentic story; your understanding, how it feels, what you've made it mean, the coping mechanisms and patterns you're noticing; and last, the gift, what you've learned, and if you wish a word of wisdom for others."

Running the circle

  • Primed before they arrive: an email goes out ahead of the circle telling people to come prepared, having already thought through their Struggle, their Understanding (the pattern or coping mechanism around it), and their Gift. The SUG frame is set before the call and reminded again during it.
  • Two roles, both named and thanked explicitly at the open: some are there to witness (not share), some are there to share. Naming both roles out loud makes the witness role feel like a real, useful contribution rather than a consolation.
  • Share your own SUG first (when they're new to you): going first models how it's done and, just as important, models brevity, so people learn how to keep it tight.
  • Timing, kept strict: frame it as 3-7 minutes, plan to nudge at 5-10. Whole circle runs about 60 minutes, as many people as you can fit. Anyone who doesn't get to share continues it inside the community (or the NLH community if coaching for NLH).
  • How to nudge: "That's amazing, Jane. Let's go ahead into the gift now. What can we say about the gift?" Moves them along without cutting them off.
  • Ground rule: no coaching, no advice. Reflections at the end of a share are personal ("this made me think...", "this, for me..."), then "does this bring something up for anyone else?" If anyone tries to coach: "We don't do coaching, let's not do advice. No coaching, no advice here." If a participant asks directly: "We don't do coaching or advice on these calls, that's what we bring into the group coaching. I know that can feel frustrating, but there's power in just sitting with the story. Troubleshoot and coach later."
  • Be present: no phone, no looking away. Engaged, and holding the witness role yourself, not just the timekeeper role.

Back pocket: if no one wants to share

You can't assume or force people to share, so have a fallback ready. A circle can legitimately become a journalling session or a conversation.

  • Share your own story first, then re-invite.
  • Do a little breath work (BEEP) to settle the room, then re-invite.
  • Turn it into a journalling round: "Take the next 10 minutes to write your struggle, the next 5 for your understanding, the next 3 for the lessons and gifts." Then: "Does anyone want to share what they wrote?"
  • Open with an allegory, a proverb, a client story (kept confidential), or a movie scene (a Harry Potter courage moment) and turn it into a conversation: "let's talk about what courage means to you."

Coaching Dialogue: The Human Rule, Empathy Rule & Curious Detective

From the module videos. Three moves, used in sequence, before any pattern work begins.

"You don't tell them this part, but what you're essentially doing is looking for the pattern they need to break. Rather than saying 'here's your pattern,' you always play the curious, questioning detective."

Three techniques for how you talk to an individual. The first two are so intuitive that most coaches never recognise them as tools at all, which is exactly why they're so powerful. Used together, they build trust and rapport fast, before any pattern work begins.

MoveWhat you sayWhy it works
1. Human Rule"You're only human. We all suffer. You're not alone." Two ways in: (a) "I've seen so many people go through something similar, you're not alone," or (b) "I've been through something similar myself. It's not the same, but you're not alone."Suffering is the one thing that binds all humans. The goal is to give them a place to feel normal, not crazy, a member of the human race rather than a broken case
2. Empathy Rule"I can see how hard that is. That must be so hard. I can't even imagine."You're not claiming you can't imagine the trauma. You're naming that you can't imagine being them, because you're not. Felt understanding without a false claim of shared experience
3. Curious Detective"I'm unsure, you can tell me I'm wrong, please correct me, but I wonder... / I think... X."Names the pattern as a hedged guess, not a verdict. Builds trust fast and keeps the client's guard down long enough to actually consider it

The premise under move 3: it's all useful

The third move rests on a belief you hold, but never announce: whatever they're going through is useful. Buried in every trauma, drama and difficulty is a lesson, a piece of wisdom, something to learn. So silently you set out to find the patterns they need to break to get those lessons. Out loud, you never say "here's your pattern." You play the curious, questioning detective instead.

Why the hedge matters

A direct assertion ("here's your pattern," "I think you're messed up") puts the client's guard up immediately. Shame walls come up before the idea even lands. A hedged offer does the opposite. "Maybe I'm off, but is it possible your mum took that comment as her being a bad mother?" The word "maybe" opens an exploratory brain state instead of a defensive one. That's real neuroplasticity: their perceptual awareness opens enough to try on a new idea. You're dropping breadcrumbs, not delivering a diagnosis.

The tone is a secondary position, not authority: "I'm clueless here, I could be way off, I have some ideas, please educate me. I'm going to push on you, but push back on me." Said that way, you'll see where they stonewall or block, but more often than not they open up with "you know... maybe." That's the whole game. Jade's name for it: the curious, humble detective and friend. Use it all the time.

Dimensionalisation of Consciousness & Parts Work

From the module videos. A Rewrite-phase perception tool. Meet a part, then integrate it.

"This is a tool for perception. It's part of the Rewrite, something you can pull out into your toolbox."

Dimensionalised consciousness is the (deliberately cumbersome) bucket name for treating an aspect of yourself as a thing you can move around inside: go deeper into it, further out of it, back into the past, or forward into the future. Once an internal experience is dimensionalised, you are no longer fused with it. You are in relationship with it. The manual covers the same stance and the Eight Doorways in Section 03.

The most common form is parts work (parts integration). Its roots aren't clinical. Jade met it first in shamanism, where the practitioner goes into a place, calls up spiritual guides and different aspects of the self, and dwells in those worlds. Internal Family Systems and modern parts work are, in this view, an extension of that shamanic lineage.

What you can dimensionalise
Selves
Past selves (child, teen, young adult) and future selves
Body parts
Down to the organ and cell: liver, kidney, cells
Memories
Specific events and scenes
Sensations
Felt in the body (tightness, heat). Distinct from feelings
Feelings / emotions
Anger, grief, fear, shame, longing
Patterns
A recurring loop or behavioural signature

Feelings and sensations are not the same thing. Feelings are emotions; sensations are what shows up in the body. Both are valid doorways.

The integration meditation (the way it's run)

Do all three at once, in one guided meditation, so the client moves through each past self in sequence rather than across separate sessions.

StepWhat happens
1. Go to the partThe client is brought to the child self, then the teen self, then the young adult (wounded adult) self
2. Dialogue & expressThey dialogue with that self and let it fully express itself
3. Safe placeThat self goes to a safe place. If they had one at that age, they go there; if not, they build one inside the meditation
4. GreetThe Next Level / current / wise self is called into the room and greets the part
5. IntegrateThe wise self integrates that part into the body. Then the whole loop repeats. Child, teen, young adult, three times

Why: the aim is to get the nervous system to recognise and respond in a new way to the different parts of our past selves. It can be run inside a session or sent home as a homework assignment.

Where it sits among the Rewrite tools

Parts work is the calm, controlled end of the toolkit. Relaxing music, soothing voice, safely revisiting places a person normally never dwells in. That is what separates it from the activating tools.

ToolStateWhat it works with
BEEPSympathetic. Intensity followed by relaxationEmotions and memories
DEEPSympathetic-parasympatheticA physical stressor: a workout, sauna therapy, cold plunge
WETWrittenWritten Exposure Therapy

In-session use: when something emotional or difficult surfaces mid-session, you can call the client to visit and dimensionalise that part in the moment, not only as a set-piece meditation.

Cross-reference to check: in this video DEEP is framed as a physical sympathetic stressor (workout, sauna, cold plunge). Section 12 of the manual defines DEEP as Depth Enhanced Emotional Processing (breathwork + subconscious scripting). Worth reconciling which sense is canonical.

Dimensionalising Consciousness: Doorways & Anthropomorphising

The companion video. Parts work is one door. This one shows all the doors, and how to turn what's behind them into a person you can talk to.

"The subconscious holds messages. It holds stories. It holds information you're not aware of. It holds MUD. If we can get them dialoguing with it rather than the conscious mind, we get material that would never have come up otherwise."

Dimensionalising is drawn from shamanism, where there are many doorways into a person's subconscious. Each doorway is a different way in, the same way one person learns best through sight, another through sound, another through movement. Parts work and Internal Family Systems are just two of these doors: they dimensionalise a historical part or an emotion. This video goes further, opening the rest.

The doorways in
Parts
Historical selves: the 8-year-old, the 28-year-old betrayed self, the exhausted 40-year-old, the angry teenager
Emotions
A feeling you dialogue with directly
Physical parts
Down to the organ and cell: liver, kidney, cells
Patterns
The overeating self, the overstriving self, the self-soothing self
Sensations
A queasiness or uneasiness in the stomach. Distinct from an emotion
Illnesses, signs & symptoms
A cancer, a painful hip. You can dialogue with these too

You bring the client in through whichever doorway is open. The goal is the same behind every door: get them talking to the subconscious.

Anthropomorphising: turn it into a person

The second half of the method. Once you've picked a doorway, you make what's behind it into an entity, a personality, a person. That gives the client a window into their own subconscious. You do it by interrogating it, then giving it a character.

AskWhat you're building
Where is it? Where does it live in the body?Location
What's its texture? Rough, smooth, like cotton?Texture
What's its temperature? What's its colour?Physical signature
If it were a person, what's its personality? A tough-love parent, a gentle partner, an angry child, an attacking monster?Personality and demeanour
What's it saying? What message does it have? What's the lesson?The payload. This is the point of the whole exercise

The physical questions (location, texture, temperature, colour) are the on-ramp. The message and the lesson are what you're actually after.

The technique in motion (worked example)

No other tool is required. You can run this on its own.

StepWhat happens
1. Close the eyesRemoves the physical stimulation all around them, so they can go inward
2. Enter through a doorwaySay it starts with a painful ankle. You go in through the physical, and describe and anthropomorphise it
3. Track where it movesAsk: is it moving anywhere? What's it telling you? The ankle may hand off to a pain in the stomach. Now interrogate the stomach: temperature, texture, personality
4. Follow it into a memoryNext thing, you're in a memory. Ask what the memory is saying. Ask: is the memory talking to the stomach or the ankle? What do these things have in common?
5. ReconsolidateOnce you're in the emotional place and getting information, you can run a reconsolidation technique

Why it changes anything: pain is a prediction

One example of reconsolidation, given without warning at the emotional peak: have the client count backward from 40 by twos. Dropping a cognitive challenge into a deeply subconscious state changes the way they hold the painful ankle.

The reason it works: pain, stories and memories are predictions made by the nervous system. They are not fixed facts. If you can change the prediction, you can change the way the body is holding the thing. That is the mechanism underneath every doorway and every reconsolidation move.

Reconsolidation is its own separate education. This video only names it. The next video in the module is "Dimensional Consciousness Technique."

Group Coaching: The 4 Ps & Compound Learning

The coach cheat sheet for running a group. How to coach a room in 60 minutes and have everyone leave coached. Video transcript to follow.

"Watch and attend as many group coaching sessions as you can. Do as many as you can. This is how you get good."

Why group beats 1:1

Compound learning. Many get coached live, but everyone in the room gets coached. Coach 6 people out of 10 and all 10 walk away with 6 viewpoints and 6 plans. Human patterns overlap, so every rep lands for more than one person. The goal never changes: shift perception to change identity and behaviour.

Pre-session email prompt

Ask everyone to arrive with the 4 Ps already filled out. The pre-work is self-coaching practice, it builds a shared language, and it makes the live session faster.

The coaching frame: the 4 Ps
PWhat it is
ProblemThe obstacle or pain. The pain point
PatternThe repeating response or coping style. How it repeats and how they cope
PossibilitiesThe lesson, the promise, the new perception. The growth hidden in the struggle
PlanThe smallest viable action now. A single step or a tiny stack

Keep bringing every share back to the 4 Ps. That is what keeps group coaching tight and scalable.

Problem vs Pattern: the core diagnostic

The coach's whole job is to separate the Problem from the Pattern, then reveal the Possibilities that bridge to a Plan. Getting these two mixed up is where coaching goes loose.

ProblemPattern
What it isThe recurrent struggle, repeated obstacle, or stuck emotionThe person's repeated response to that problem
Looks likeCore needs implicated: Safety & Security, Acceptance & Belonging, Freedom & Autonomy, Growth & Mattering. Usually in Money, Health, Relationships or PurposePeople-pleasing, denial, distraction, whimper, whine, avoid, attack

The three coping clusters (the Pattern side):

Soothing
Food, alcohol, sex, screens
Striving
Overwork, overtraining, nonstop doing
Seeking
Obsessive why-hunting, analysis loops

These three are the same Soother / Striver / Seeker identity traps used across the recovery work. For Alex, the Soother cluster (screens, sex) is usually the presenting Pattern, sitting on top of a Problem in Relationships or Growth & Mattering.

The Possibilities move

This is where the reframe happens. Weave in the NLH assumptions: the person is unique, they have unique work to do, and life presents them tailored struggles to awaken that work. Then bridge it for them:

"Here is your problem and your pattern. Here is what you want. The lessons inside this struggle are the missing bridge."

Session flow

Drop-in phase. Pick one brief opener: BEEP or simple breathwork (aim for alpha, theta if possible), a journaling prompt on the 4 Ps, or a short teaching bite, movement or art activity. The opener does two jobs: it primes the subconscious, and it is your fallback if nobody volunteers.

Live coaching rounds. Call a volunteer, walk them through the 4 Ps, keep it time-bound, then repeat with new volunteers.

When the room is big, run a guided journaling sequence first, then take 2 to 4 volunteers for quick laser rounds:

TimePrompt
5-10 minProblem: sensations, thoughts, feelings, choices, actions
5-10 minPattern: recurring coping, major and minor
5-7 minPossibilities: lessons, promises, growth
2-5 minPlan: single step or tiny stack

Running the container

Priming the room, strict timing and a clean close are what keep the container powerful and safe.
