◈ NLH Framework Overview
"Identity-level change is the only change that lasts. All behaviour, emotion, and physiology is downstream of identity."

The SIGNAL cascade runs: Identity → Nervous System → Hormones → Immune Function → Behaviour. Every automatic pattern a client presents with is at the bottom of this cascade. The coaching error is to target behaviour and call it change.

Behaviour is the output. The identity structure generating it is the problem. When you treat the output as the problem, you're working on a symptom while the cause continues running at full strength.

RewriteStory / Belief / DMNChanges the narrative identity is running. Targets the meaning layer.MUD Rewrite, WET, Chapter 10
RewireEmotional charge / bodyClears the emotional energy fused to the story. Targets the body, not the mind.DEEP, BEEP, QEC, breathwork
RetrainBehaviour / nervous systemBuilds new lived patterns through repetition until the new path fires faster than the old one.VIR, Fear PRs, Chapter 10, WER Loop

Rewrite without Rewire = insight without embodiment. The story shifts but the emotional charge regenerates it. The client understands and still acts the old way.

Rewire without Rewrite = temporary relief. The charge releases but the story regenerates it. All three phases must be addressed, in sequence.

MUD = Misguided Unconscious Decision. The core unit of conditioned identity. Formula: Stress Event + Story + Emotion = MUD

MUD forms when a stressful experience generates a judgment, which crystallises into a simple absolute story ("I am not enough"), which fuses with emotion under stress, and hardens into an identity structure the brain now defends as real.

MUD is not pathology. It was adaptive at the time. The problem is that it calcified into the nervous system's operating assumption about who this person is — and the whole cascade runs from that assumption.

MUD hardening spectrum: fluid → clay → cement. Pre-verbal MUD is the deepest. Depth determines the intervention intensity required, not the severity of the original event.

◈ From the Manual
"We are not suppressing patterns. We are not overriding them. We are working within the reconsolidation window to update the underlying assembly itself."

Every automatic pattern — the urge, the pull, the compulsion — is a neural assembly: a network of neurons that fires as a unit whenever any part of it is triggered (Hebb's Law). That assembly was built through repetition. Deciding differently does not change it. The wiring is where the work must happen.

The reconsolidation window: Consolidated memories are not permanently fixed. When retrieved into active attention, they become temporarily unstable for 4–6 hours. During that labile window, the memory can be updated. The original assembly is not erased — it is re-encoded with new information.

Three things are required for reconsolidation to work. If any are missing, the intervention fails:

StepWhat it meansWhy it matters
1. RetrievalBring the assembly into active attentionThe window only opens when the memory is live and emotionally activated
2. Prediction ErrorCreate a mismatch between what the assembly expects and what the client experiencesThis is the trigger. No mismatch = window doesn't open = no update
3. New EncodingIntroduce new content during the labile windowMust happen before re-stabilisation. This is what changes the assembly.

The four channels of prediction error the BRN protocols use simultaneously:

  • Somatic: body expected to react one way; it doesn't (the watcher stance and breath hold the client in a different state)
  • Cognitive: pattern was encoded as "this is who I am"; new frame asks "what is your lesson? What are you here to teach?"
  • Perceptual: sensory context (music, breath, eye mask) contradicts the context in which the pattern was reinforced
  • Identity: the deepest channel. Watcher stance breaks identification — "I have this pattern, I am not this pattern." This is what makes the BRN protocols different from behaviour management.

"The watcher is how the client meets what arises. Dimensionalising is what they do once they meet it."

Watcher Stance: The shift from being-the-experience to being-the-one-watching-the-experience. "I notice anger here" — not "I am angry." This is what makes deeper subconscious work safe. Without it, retrieval risks re-traumatisation. With it, retrieval becomes the doorway to updating.

In daily life this is what Chapter 10 practice builds in real time: What am I about to do? What do I usually do next? What would my Next Level Self do instead? The watcher in the moment before the old path activates.

Dimensionalising: Once the watcher stance is established, treat the internal experience as a separate entity. Give it a location, a colour, a texture, a temperature, a shape, a voice. What is it trying to say? What is its lesson?

Once an experience has been dimensionalised, the client is no longer fused with it. They are in relationship with it. From relationship, the work can happen. This is what makes the MUD Rewrite process feel different from journalling — the client is meeting the pattern as an entity, not analysing it. That distinction produces the identity-level prediction error the assembly needs to destabilise.

The Eight Doorways — entry points into the subconscious. One usually presents first in a session. The coach follows what arises:

  • Historical parts (child self, teen self, the wounded version)
  • Emotional parts (anger, grief, fear, shame — dimensionalised)
  • Body parts (heart, gut, throat, shoulders)
  • Physical sensations (tightness, heaviness, tingling, warmth)
  • Patterns (the recurring loop itself — what is it? what does it want?)
  • Memories (specific events and scenes)
  • Signs and symptoms (the body's physical expressions of held material)
  • Illnesses and diagnoses

"Every BRN protocol is built on one of two attentional modes. FA is the channel. OM is the field. Knowing which you are using tells you exactly what the protocol is doing to the nervous system."

Focused Attention (FA): Direct, narrow, sustained attention onto a specific object. Activates dorsolateral prefrontal cortex, anterior cingulate, and task-positive networks. In NLH terms: channel-based work. You tune the receiver to one station.

Open Monitoring (OM): Broad, non-selective awareness in which whatever arises is observed without targeting. Activates the Default Mode Network and posterior integrative regions. In NLH terms: field-based work. You open the receiver to the entire spectrum at once. (Lutz, Slagter, Dunne & Davidson, 2008)

The Coherence Breath: Six-second inhale paired with six-second exhale. No holds. Sustained as a continuous rhythm. Produces respiratory sinus arrhythmia at its maximal coherence point — measurably improves heart-rate variability, reduces stress markers, produces a balanced autonomic state. (McCraty & Childre, 2010; Lehrer & Gevirtz, 2014)

Two clinical uses: (1) The safe alternative for clients who cannot use the activating breath — all BRN protocols can be run with coherence breath substitution. (2) The integration breath — used during journaling phases, reflection, and standalone homework practice.

Vocalization and sound: Humming reliably increases nasal nitric oxide, activates the vagus nerve via the laryngeal branch, and produces parasympathetic dominance. OM chanting studies show decreased limbic activity and increased heart-rate variability. (Eby, 2006; Kalyani et al., 2011)

Clinical uses in BRN: the hum on the breaking breath; sustained chakra tones in QEC variations (root through crown: uh, oh, ah, ay, ee, ohm, mmm); OM-style sustained tone at the close of QAT.

"This is not coaching. Instead, what you are doing is facilitating a conversation, or facilitating a witnessing. In the authentic telling of the story of struggle, healing is done."

Story circles (also called conversation circles) are a distinct delivery format alongside 1:1 coaching, group coaching, and BRN classes — not a variant of any of them. The model takes its inspiration from 12-step: the mechanism that matters is story, and being witnessed in the telling of it.

The clinical link back to reconsolidation: retrieving a memory makes it labile — loosely held and changeable (see Coaching Methods 01). When someone is prompted to tell their story slightly differently — naming the pattern, naming a gift — the story itself begins to change as they hold it. The circle is a reconsolidation event delivered through group witnessing rather than a protocol.

SUG StepWhat they tellWhy it's asked
StruggleThe authentic story — what happenedThe raw material. Told straight, without performance.
UnderstandingWhat the struggle means, coping mechanisms and patterns noticedMoves them from event to pattern-recognition
GiftWhat they've learned, a word of wisdom for someone earlier in the same struggleMost people don't see a gift in their struggle unfacilitated. Naming one is the reconsolidation move — it changes how the story is held, not just how it's told.

Two roles, both named and thanked explicitly at the open: some are there to witness (not share), some are there to share. Naming both roles out loud matters — it makes the witness role feel like a real, useful contribution rather than a consolation.

Ground rule: no coaching, no advice. If a participant asks for it directly, the line is: "We don't do coaching or advice on these calls — I know that can feel frustrating, but there's power in just sitting with the story. Troubleshoot and coach later." Facilitator reflections at the end of a share are personal ("this made me think..."), never directive.

Timing: 3–7 minutes framed, nudge at 5–10 minutes if needed ("That's amazing, Jane — let's go ahead into the gift now"). Full circle runs ~60 minutes; anyone who doesn't get to share continues it inside the community.

Back pocket, if no one volunteers to share: facilitator shares their own SUG first to model brevity and structure, or moves the group into a journalling round (write the SUG instead of speaking it), then re-invites sharing. An allegory, proverb, or shared scene (a movie moment, a client story kept confidential) can also open the conversation without forcing disclosure.

Facilitator must be fully present — no phone, no looking away — and holds the witness role too, not just the timekeeper role.

Three moves, used in sequence, before any pattern work begins:

MoveWhat you sayWhy it works
1. Human Rule"You're not alone — I've seen so many people go through this," or "I've been through something similar myself"Normalises. Every human suffers; the goal is to make the client feel like a member of the human race, not a broken case
2. Empathy Rule"I can't even imagine — that must be so hard"You're not claiming to imagine the trauma itself — you're naming that you can't imagine being them. Felt understanding without false claims of shared experience
3. Curious Detective"I could be completely wrong, correct me — but I wonder if... / I think..."Names the pattern as a hedged guess, not a verdict. Keeps the client's guard down long enough to actually consider it

Why the hedge matters: a direct assertion ("here's your pattern," "you're messed up about this") puts the client's guard up immediately — shame walls come up before the idea even lands. A hedged offer ("maybe I'm off, but is it possible your mum took that comment as her being a bad mother?") does the opposite: "maybe" opens an exploratory brain state instead of a defensive one, dropping the idea in as a breadcrumb rather than a diagnosis. The client can pick it up, push back on it, or let it sit — either way, the door stays open, which a flat assertion closes.

Practical pattern: "I'm unsure, you can tell me I'm wrong, please correct me — but I wonder / I think [pattern observation]." Said in that hedging, secondary-position tone, not an authoritative one.

This is why "be it until you see it" (not fake it till you make it) is neurobiologically accurate. Identity precedes behaviour because the nervous system is always acting in line with its current identity prediction. Change the identity, and behaviour follows.

"Regulation is NOT relaxation. Relaxation soothes the moment. Regulation builds the capacity to function inside activation."

MUD was formed under sympathetic activation. High arousal seared the story into the system through limbic engagement and stress hormone signalling. If the story was cemented under activation, it cannot be fully reshaped in a state of sedation.

The oscillation is the mechanism: Activate → Reframe → Recover

  • Activation: deliberately enter the encoding state (the nervous system goes up)
  • Reframe: introduce meaning shift WHILE activated (cognitive flexibility under load)
  • Recovery: slow exhale, self-touch, genuine elevated emotion — the system returns, but to a new baseline

Each cycle deposits a new lived experience: "I can enter this state and come back differently." Repeated across many cycles, this rewrites the baseline autonomic tone. This is earned regulation, not relaxation.

The wellness world conflates regulation with relaxation. NLH does not. Relaxation soothes the moment. Regulation builds the capacity to function inside activation — to remain in the charged state long enough for new content to encode. These are fundamentally different outcomes.

The High Self vs The Parts: The person is not a single unified self. They are a cast of internal characters, each formed around a specific wound. Personal Relationships

The neuroscience and psychology of human connection. Subconscious knots, attachment, the AFRAID stack, reciprocity, Confident Detachment, social architecture, and the ten moves of relational change.

◈ From the Manual
"Every relational pattern is a downstream expression of identity structures installed during the three developmental windows."

The ball of yarn metaphor: every relationship pattern is a tangle of threads that leads back to a single seed story. You can pull at the surface thread (the current argument, the distance, the recurring rupture) for years without reaching the knot. Or you can locate the knot and unravel it from the source.

Relational knots are tied within three developmental windows. The window determines the theme of the wound. The coach who knows which window is active can quickly narrow the territory of the work.

WindowAge RangeCore Wound Theme
ChildhoodBirth - 10Safety and security. "Can the world be trusted? Am I safe to be vulnerable?"
Adolescent11 - 20Belonging and acceptance. "Do I fit? Am I enough for the group? Do I deserve connection?"
Adult20+Betrayal and abandonment. "Can I trust specific people with my full self? What happens when intimacy breaks?"

Window age is the developmental origin of the MUD, not the current age of the client. A 45-year-old client whose presenting wound is abandonment may be working on material encoded during an adolescent rejection. Identify the window, trace the seed story, work from there.

"Every presenting emotion points toward the one beneath it. Address the surface without tracing the stack and you produce temporary relief and re-entrenchment."

Fear PRs (Personal Records) are graduated behavioural challenges in the direction of the fear. In training, a PR is best performance on a specific lift. In relational coaching, it is best performance in a specific fear domain: doing the scary thing at a manageable level, notching the rep, then raising the bar over time.

Examples:

  • Making eye contact with a stranger (social anxiety)
  • Expressing a preference when asked (Pleaser pattern)
  • Saying no to a small request (approval-seeker)
  • Initiating a vulnerable conversation about one specific topic (avoidant)

The mechanism: the nervous system learns safety through repeated survivable experience, not through argument or intention. Fear PRs are designed exposures that build the biological evidence of safety the nervous system requires before it will update its neuroception.

Every significant relationship contains three distinct entities. The coaching error is to focus on only one or two of them.

EntityWhat It Is

Ensemble (E)The third entity: the relationship itself, with its own patterns, rituals, energy, and dynamics that exist independent of either individual.

The 100/100 Principle: Healthy relationships are not 50/50. Each person brings 100% of their authentic self, takes 100% responsibility for their contribution to the dynamic, and operates from 100% genuine engagement. 50/50 means each person is withholding half of themselves, and the relationship runs on a deficit from the start.

Differentiation vs Fusion:

  • Fusion: Partners lose individual identity in service of relational harmony. The "we" swallows the "you" and the "me." The relationship feels close but is built on self-disappearance, not connection.
  • Differentiation: Partners maintain full individual identity while being genuinely connected. The relationship is stronger because neither person has disappeared into it. Two fully present people creating a third thing together.
