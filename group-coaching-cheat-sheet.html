Group Coaching — Coach Cheat Sheet

Standalone quick reference for running a Next Level Human group coaching session. Not the video transcript — for the full working detail, worked examples and sample scripts, see Section 18 of the Coaching Methods manual.

Why group beats 1:1

Compound learning. Many get coached, all get coached. Ten people, coach 6 live: everyone receives 6 viewpoints and plans. The overlap in human patterns means every rep lands for multiple people. The goal remains the same: shift perception to change identity and behaviour.

Pre-session email prompt

Ask participants to arrive with the 4 Ps filled out.

  • Problem — the pain point
  • Pattern — how it repeats and how they cope
  • Possibilities / Promises — the growth opportunity hidden in the struggle
  • Plan — the single next action or small stack of actions

Purpose of the pre-work: self-coaching practice, a shared language, faster live sessions.

The coaching frame: the 4 Ps

PWhat it is
ProblemThe obstacle or pain
PatternThe repeating response or coping style
PossibilitiesThe lesson, promise, new perception
PlanSmallest viable action now

Keep bringing every share back to the 4 Ps. It keeps coaching tight and scalable.

Session flow options

Drop-in phase — choose one opener: BEEP or simple breathwork (aim for alpha, theta if possible), a journaling prompt on the 4 Ps, or a short teaching bite / movement / art activity. Use it to prime the subconscious, and as your fallback if no one volunteers.

Live coaching rounds — call a volunteer, walk the 4 Ps, keep it time-bound, repeat.

When the room is big — run a guided journaling sequence first, then take 2-4 volunteers for quick laser rounds:

TimePrompt
5-10 minProblem: sensations, thoughts, feelings, choices, actions
5-10 minPattern: recurring coping, major and minor
5-7 minPossibilities: lessons, promises, growth
2-5 minPlan: single step or tiny stack

Problem vs Pattern: fast diagnostics

Problem — recurrent struggle, repeated obstacle, stuck emotion. Core needs often implicated: Safety & Security, Acceptance & Belonging, Freedom & Autonomy, Growth & Mattering. Common life arenas: Money, Health, Relationships, Purpose.

Pattern — the person's repeated response to the problem. People-pleasing, denial, distraction, whimper, whine, avoid, attack.

Soothing
Food, alcohol, sex, screens
Striving
Overwork, overtraining, nonstop doing
Seeking
Obsessive why-hunting, analysis loops

Coach's job: separate Problem from Pattern, then reveal the Possibilities that bridge to a Plan.

The Possibilities move

NLH assumptions to weave in: they are unique; they have unique work; source consciousness presents tailored struggles to awaken that work.

"Here is your problem and pattern, here is what you want, the lessons inside this struggle are the missing bridge."

Timekeeping

  • Sessions run 60 minutes, end on time
  • Individual shares 3-7 minutes each, cap at ~10
  • If someone runs long, pivot to: "What is your gift or plan now?"

If no volunteers

  • Use the opener as the main event: breathwork or journaling on the 4 Ps
  • Then invite 1-2 brief shares

Closing

  • Recap insights quickly
  • Direct those who didn't get coached to post in the community, or to next session
  • Re-affirm the 60-minute boundary — clarity builds trust

Key takeaways

  • Group coaching multiplies impact through compound learning
  • The 4 Ps make sessions fast, deep, and repeatable
  • Distinguish Problem from Pattern, then unlock Possibilities and a Plan
  • Priming the room, strict timing, clean closing keeps the container powerful and safe

"Watch and attend as many sessions as you can. Do as many sessions as you can. This is how you get good."
