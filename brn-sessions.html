The Session Rules

Dosing and sequencing. Read these before anything else on this page.

Three rules, no exceptions

  1. Do at least one type per week. BRN work is a weekly minimum, not an occasional extra.
  2. Best sequence: DELTA or QAT on the days before AND after DEEP or QEC. Bracket the heavy sessions with the gentle ones.
  3. DEEP and QEC: once per week each, maximum. DELTA and QAT: as often as you wish.

Why the limits exist: DEEP and QEC are the intensive sessions. They surface and move buried emotional material, and the system needs time to integrate what they open up. DELTA and QAT are the preparation and integration sessions, so there is no cap on them.

What BRN Sessions Are

Belief Repatterning and Neuroplasticity: the cornerstone of identity transformation at Next Level Human

BRN stands for Belief Repatterning and Neuroplasticity Sessions. They are immersive, science-meets-soul experiences that access and reprogram the subconscious mind.

At their core, BRN Sessions target the beliefs that unconsciously shape identity and behavior. These beliefs usually arise from the emotional judgments and interpretations formed at the inception of MUD (Misguided Unconscious Decisions). Those judgments begin as choices. Over time they solidify into ingrained belief structures that shape perception and physiology alike. BRN Sessions unravel and reprogram these beliefs through conscious intervention and neuroplastic remodeling.

This is not traditional meditation or breathwork. BRN Sessions are targeted transformation protocols designed to dismantle MUD by doing three things at once:

  • Altering brainwave states
  • Shifting emotional energy
  • Rewriting the inner narratives that shape identity

Every BRN Session changes perception, dissolves outdated patterns, and opens new possibilities for being. Whether you are confronting trauma, breaking old belief systems, or stepping into your next level of self, these sessions offer a precise and powerful path.

The Paradigm Behind BRN

Consciousness is primary. The brain is the receiver, not the source.

Consciousness is primary

BRN Sessions are built on a core claim: consciousness is primary. Outdated models assume the brain creates consciousness. Next Level Human aligns with a growing number of philosophers, psychologists, and physicists who view consciousness as the foundational energetic scaffolding of reality. Not a byproduct of the brain, but the field that informs it.

The brain as receiver

In this paradigm the brain is not the source of consciousness, it is the receiver. Much like a radio tunes to a frequency, the brain receives, organizes, and expresses consciousness through electrochemical patterns.

The biofield and CPNEI

The biofield is the energetic interface between consciousness and the body. It translates the vibrational intelligence of consciousness into the Conscious-Psycho-Neuroendocrine-Immune (CPNEI) system, the integrated, responsive network of biology, emotion, and perception. By working at the junction between the quantum field (consciousness) and the classical world (biochemistry and neurology), BRN Sessions initiate transformation from the top down and the inside out.

Belief change and neuroplasticity reinforce each other

BRN Sessions operate on the principle that belief change and neuroplasticity are not separate but mutually reinforcing. Changing beliefs drives neurological restructuring, and those neural shifts in turn reinforce new patterns of thought, emotion, and behavior.

The sessions rewire the energetic body and the physical body simultaneously. They adjust belief structures, clear emotional and energetic holding patterns, and reshape the physiological architecture of the brain itself, the process known as neuroplasticity. Neuroplasticity becomes the physical mirror of what is happening energetically: the expansion of identity, the clearing of MUD, and the restoration of possibility.

Why this matters for physical health: this energetic-to-physical healing is particularly important for chronic health issues, especially neurological concerns like brain fog, memory loss, trauma-related symptoms, and emotional dysregulation. Because the body truly keeps the score, BRN Sessions help resolve the physiological imprint of unresolved emotional stress. Clearing the energetic distortion in the biofield while supporting neuroplasticity in the brain makes these sessions a bridge between emotional release and physical healing.

The Four Pillars of BRN

DEEP, QEC, QAT, DELTA: what each one does and when to use it

1. DEEP · Depth Enhanced Emotional Processing

Primary function: Rewriting and Rewiring

DEEP is the most advanced transformational experience in the system. It uses BEEP (Breath Enhanced Emotional Processing) to stimulate the sympathetic nervous system and move emotional energy that has been buried or stuck. Through a guided sequence of breathwork, subconscious scripting, dimensionalized consciousness, and journaling, DEEP works like a psychological scalpel, cutting through layers of MUD to release and reprogram the unconscious.

Think of DEEP as the ultimate identity disruptor. It brings the knotted stories to the surface, then helps dissolve and rewire them.

"DEEP is for when you're ready to break cycles, not just understand them."

Ideal for: breaking emotional patterns, major life reinvention, identity shifts, deep trauma work.

2. QEC · Quantum Energy Clearing

Primary function: Rewriting and Energetic Cleansing

QEC works through the chakra system, the energetic blueprint of the human body. Each session focuses on a specific energy center, using guided visualization, somatic awareness, and subconscious recalibration to clear emotional blocks. This is where belief meets biology.

QEC is especially potent for releasing trauma stored in the body and for health conditions with emotional roots. It is a mental-emotional hygiene practice, a full-body energy reset for the psyche.

"The body keeps the score. QEC clears it."

Ideal for: health issues, energetic stagnation, emotional toxicity, chronic patterns rooted in survival programming.

3. QAT · Quantum Awareness Training

Primary function: Attunement to Source and Perception Expansion

QAT is a nothingness meditation. It could also be described as a "manifestation meditation." It teaches you how to access Source Consciousness by stepping into a state of pure awareness. It is not about doing, it is about being. As you align your nervous system with infinite intelligence, you begin to perceive synchronicity, insight, and guidance that were always there but filtered out by unconscious noise.

QAT develops your capacity to live from the inside out. To trust the field. To access clarity in stillness.

"QAT opens you to the you beyond the noise."

Ideal for: enhancing intuition, creative insight, decision-making, and accessing non-ordinary states of awareness.

4. DELTA · Downregulation & Nervous System Restoration

Primary function: Rewiring and Restoration

DELTA works with the deepest healing states of the brain, helping reset the nervous system from chronic fight-or-flight. Through brainwave entrainment, guided rest-state meditations, and deep parasympathetic activation, DELTA helps restore equilibrium, integrate emotional breakthroughs, and build a new baseline of safety.

If DEEP is the fire, DELTA is the water.

"You can't rewire while in survival mode. DELTA makes healing possible by creating safety in the system."

Ideal for: nervous system reset, integrating breakthroughs from heavier sessions, and building a baseline of safety.

BEEP: Full Directions

Breath Enhanced Emotional Processing, the 11-minute practice inside DEEP. Everything needed to run a session is on this page.

What is BEEP?

BEEP is an 11-minute breath practice designed to help you process and transform emotions at the deepest level. It works by alternating between two types of breath:

  • Activating Breath to bring forward difficult sensations, memories, or emotions.
  • Braking Breath to anchor positive, loving states that transmute those difficulties into insight and peace.

The process is rooted in rest-based breathing: breathe until you can't, rest until you can. You control the intensity at all times.

Setup

  • Choose a quiet place where you will not be disturbed.
  • Use headphones and an eye mask if available.
  • Sit upright or lie down. Never standing or driving.
  • Best times: first thing in the morning or before bed, though any time will work.
  • Session length: about 11 minutes.

The Two Breathing Modes

Activating (Accelerating) Breath
Braking (Recovery) Breath
Mechanics: two quick inhales (belly, then chest), followed by a passive exhale.
Mechanics: one deep, full inhale, followed by a very slow, controlled exhale, preferably with a hum or vocalization.
Purpose: invite forward difficult material: memories, sensations, emotions, even fear.
Purpose: call in feelings of love, gratitude, compassion, safety.
Attitude: greet whatever arises as a friend and teacher. Ask silently: "What are you here to show me?"
Attitude: imagine bathing your body in healing light and warmth.

Step-by-Step Practice (11 Minutes)

Minute 0–1 · Arrival

Close your eyes, relax your body, and set an intention.

Whisper inwardly: "I am open to meeting what arises with love and awareness."

Minute 1–7 · Activating Phase

Begin the activating breath: double inhale, passive exhale.

Breathe as intensely or gently as feels right.

Rest whenever you need to with the braking breath, then return to activating.

As difficult sensations or emotions surface, welcome them: "I see you. I welcome you. Teach me."

Minute 7–11 · Braking Phase

Transition fully into braking breath: big inhale, long humming exhale.

Let the exhale stretch as far as it naturally will.

With each breath, imagine filling your body with compassion, safety, and love.

Anchor this as your new baseline state.

Completion

Let your breath return to normal.

Gently move your fingers and toes.

Place one hand on your heart, take a final deep breath, and express gratitude for the session.

What to Expect

  • Shedding responses: yawning, tears, shaking, goosebumps, temperature changes. These are signs of release.
  • Movement: your body may want to sway, stretch, or shake. Allow it.
  • Emotional waves: sadness, fear, anger, joy. Welcome all as teachers.
  • Symbols / memories: images or intuitive insights may arise.

Integration

  • Whatever arises, pleasant or difficult, is material for growth.
  • Treat it as medicine, not a mistake.
  • After finishing, you may want to journal, stretch, or simply sit in silence.
